New Zealand offers a high quality of life, internationally recognized qualifications, and a relatively straightforward immigration pathway for skilled graduates. For Indian students, the combination of work rights during study and a clear post-study work visa makes New Zealand an attractive alternative to Australia — with smaller class sizes and a friendlier environment.

How to Get a New Zealand Student Visa

  1. Apply and get accepted at a New Zealand university or polytechnic (Fee-paying student)
  2. Get an Offer of Place (enrollment confirmation) from your institution
  3. Apply online through the Immigration New Zealand (INZ) portal
  4. Upload documents: offer of place, financial proof, health declaration, English proficiency scores
  5. Pay NZD $330 visa application fee
  6. Biometrics may be required at a Visa Application Centre in India
  7. Receive decision in 4–6 weeks

Work Rights During Study

New Zealand student visas automatically include work rights for most full-time degree programs:

  • 20 hours per week during semester
  • Full-time during scheduled breaks (summer, semester breaks)
  • Spouses of students enrolled in courses lasting 6+ months can also get open work visas

Post-Study Work Visa (PSWV)

  • Bachelor's degree or higher (3+ years): 3-year open work visa
  • Diploma or bachelor's (2–3 years): 2-year open work visa
  • Certificate or diploma (1–2 years): 1-year open work visa
  • Work in any job for any employer — no sponsorship required

Key Tips for Indian Students

  • IELTS 6.0+ (overall band) is the standard requirement for most institutions
  • New Zealand has a skills shortage list — nursing, IT, engineering, and trades graduates have strong PR pathways
  • Accommodation: apply for on-campus housing early as places are limited
  • Auckland has the largest Indian community; Christchurch and Wellington are smaller but cheaper to live in
  • New Zealand's Skilled Migrant Category (SMC) lets graduates with job offers apply for permanent residency
