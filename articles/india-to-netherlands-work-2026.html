The Netherlands is one of Europe's top tech hubs, home to ASML, Booking.com, Philips, and hundreds of international startups. For Indian tech professionals, the Highly Skilled Migrant (Kennismigrant) visa offers a fast-track work permit — with one of the most favourable tax benefits in Europe: the 30% ruling.

The 30% Ruling: Tax Benefit for Expats

The Netherlands offers an extraordinary tax benefit for international hires: the 30% ruling allows eligible expats to receive 30% of their gross salary tax-free for the first 5 years of employment.

For example, on a €70,000 salary, only €49,000 is taxed — effectively reducing your tax burden by 10–15 percentage points. This makes Dutch salaries more competitive than the headline figures suggest.

Note: The 30% ruling has been subject to legislative changes — verify current rules at the Belastingdienst website.

Step-by-Step Process

  1. Secure a job offer from a Dutch company that is an IND-recognized sponsor
  2. Employer files the HSM permit application with IND on your behalf
  3. IND decides in 2–5 weeks
  4. Receive your residence permit (combined work + residence) — valid for the duration of your employment contract
  5. Apply for 30% ruling within 4 months of starting work (via your employer)
  6. After 5 years: apply for permanent residency

Dutch Tech Job Market for Indians

  • Amsterdam: fintech, e-commerce (Booking.com, Adyen), startups
  • Eindhoven: semiconductor and hardware (ASML, NXP, Philips)
  • Delft / The Hague: government tech, cybersecurity, research
  • English is the official business language in most tech companies
  • Average software engineer salary: €55,000–€90,000/year

Key Tips

  • Most Dutch tech companies actively hire internationally — apply directly via LinkedIn or company career pages
  • Housing in Amsterdam is extremely competitive — start looking before your visa is approved
  • Learn basic Dutch — not required, but appreciated and opens social doors
  • Register with your municipality (gemeente) within 5 days of arriving — required by law
  • BSN (citizen service number) is needed to open a bank account — get this from gemeente first
