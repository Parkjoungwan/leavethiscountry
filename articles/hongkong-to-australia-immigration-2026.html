Australia launched specific immigration pathways for Hong Kong residents in 2021 following events in Hong Kong. These, combined with Australia's established points-based skilled migration system, make Australia one of the top 3 destinations for Hong Kong emigrants alongside the UK and Canada. Sydney has the world's second-largest Cantonese-speaking community outside Asia.

HK-Specific Australian Visa Streams

  • Subclass 403 – Hong Kong: Temporary work visa for HKSAR passport holders — up to 5 years, any employer
  • Subclass 191 pathway: After 3 years in a regional area on an HK work visa, eligible for permanent residency via the Regional pathway
  • Australian government also waived certain fees and expedited processing for HK applicants in 2021–2023 periods

HK Advantages in Australian Points Test

  • Strong English proficiency (IELTS 8+ common for HK applicants) = 20 points
  • Age 25–32 = 30 points (peak earning years)
  • Bachelor's degree = 15 points
  • Australian education or work experience = 5–10 bonus points
  • Finance, accounting, IT, engineering — all on Australia's skilled occupations list

HK Community in Australia

  • Sydney: Chatswood, Hurstville, Burwood — major Cantonese-speaking suburbs; yum cha culture thriving
  • Melbourne: Box Hill, Glen Waverley — extensive Chinese/Cantonese community
  • HK-style cafes (cha chaan teng), BBQ pork shops, bubble tea, and Cantonese newspapers all widely available
  • Cantonese TV channels and radio stations broadcast in Sydney and Melbourne
