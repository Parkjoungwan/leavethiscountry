Australia is one of the top three destinations for Indian students, with over 100,000 Indian students enrolled annually. The country offers strong work rights during study, a clear post-study pathway, and one of the largest Indian communities outside India. The main challenge is cost — it's one of the more expensive destinations — and the GTE (Genuine Temporary Entrant) requirement, which has led to increased refusals in recent years.

The GTE Requirement: What It Is and Why It Gets People Rejected

The Genuine Temporary Entrant (GTE) requirement means you must demonstrate you intend to study in Australia temporarily and return home afterward. The Department of Home Affairs assesses:

  • Your personal circumstances in India (family, property, employment ties)
  • Why you chose Australia specifically over other countries
  • Why you chose your specific institution and course
  • Your immigration history
  • Your study background and how the Australian degree fits your career goals

Indian students with weak GTE statements — or who appear to be using study as a path to staying permanently — face increased refusal rates. A strong, specific GTE statement is essential.

Work Rights: 48 Hours Per Fortnight

During your studies, you can work 48 hours per fortnight (effectively full-time during holidays, part-time during semester). This is a significant income source — at AUD $23.23/hour minimum wage, 48 hours/fortnight = approximately AUD $30,000/year extra income potential.
