Portugal has emerged as a top relocation destination for Koreans seeking a slower pace of life in Europe — with affordable living costs, a warm climate, and one of the fastest paths to EU citizenship. Korea-Portugal ties are growing, and a small but active Korean community has established itself in Lisbon and Porto. The D7 and Digital Nomad visas are the most popular routes for Korean nationals.

Why Koreans Choose Portugal

  • Cost of living: significantly lower than Seoul — rent in Porto is 50–60% cheaper than Gangnam
  • EU access: after 5 years, Portuguese citizenship grants visa-free access to 180+ countries
  • Safe environment: Portugal consistently ranks among Europe's safest countries
  • English widely spoken: especially in Lisbon and Porto among younger generations
  • Korean community: small but growing, with Korean restaurants and cultural events in Lisbon

D7 Visa Application Process

  1. Open a Portuguese bank account remotely (or after arriving on tourist visa)
  2. Secure accommodation in Portugal (rental contract or property deed)
  3. Obtain Korean criminal background check (apostilled and translated)
  4. Apply at Portuguese consulate with income proof (pension, dividends, rental income, savings)
  5. Receive 4-month visa; travel to Portugal
  6. Schedule AIMA appointment for 2-year residence permit

Monthly Cost of Living in Portugal

  • Lisbon (1BR apartment): €1,200–€1,800/month total
  • Porto: €900–€1,400/month total
  • Algarve coast: €800–€1,300/month (popular retirement destination)
  • Korean grocery items available at Asian supermarkets; Korean restaurant options expanding
