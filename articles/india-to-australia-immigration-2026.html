Australia's permanent residency pathway is one of the most structured in the world — but also one of the most competitive for Indian applicants. The points-based system rewards age, English, education, and work experience. Indian applicants frequently score well on paper, but competition from other nationalities and state nomination limits make the process genuinely difficult.

Reality check: An Indian applicant with a bachelor's degree, 5 years experience, age 28, IELTS 7.0, and state nomination would score roughly 80–85 points. Most 190 invitations currently require 80–90 points depending on the state and occupation.

Skills Assessment: The First Hurdle

Before you can even submit an Expression of Interest (EOI), you need a positive skills assessment from the relevant assessing body:

  • Engineers: Engineers Australia (EA)
  • IT professionals: Australian Computer Society (ACS)
  • Accountants: CPA Australia, CAANZ, or IPA
  • Nurses: AHPRA
  • Teachers: AITSL

Skills assessments take 4–12 weeks and cost AUD $400–$1,000. Your occupation must also be on the relevant skilled occupation list.

Total Cost Estimate

  • Skills assessment: AUD $400–$1,000
  • English test (IELTS): AUD $375
  • State nomination application fee: AUD $0–$300 (varies by state)
  • Visa application fee (subclass 189/190): AUD $4,640 (primary) + $2,325 (partner) + $1,160 (each child)
  • Medical exams: AUD $300–$600
  • Police clearances: AUD $42–$100

Why It Rates "Hard" for Indian Applicants

  • Annual immigration cap limits total PR grants — many occupations have long queues
  • Indian applicants don't receive any country-specific advantage in the points test
  • State nomination is discretionary — states prioritize occupations in local demand
  • Age cutoff of 45 is strict — points drop sharply after 33
  • Processing times of 1–3 years create uncertainty
