Singapore is Asia's financial and tech hub — and one of the most popular work destinations for Indian professionals. The Employment Pass (EP) is employer-sponsored, processes in 3–8 weeks, and gives access to one of the world's highest-paying job markets. Indians are the second-largest foreign workforce in Singapore, with a strong presence in banking, tech, and consulting.

Employment Pass: The Main Work Visa

The Employment Pass (EP) is for professionals, managers, executives, and technicians (PMET). Key requirements:

  • Minimum salary: SGD $5,000/month (higher for financial services sector: SGD $5,500)
  • Education: Degree from a recognized university required
  • Employer applies: Your employer submits the EP application via MyMOM Portal — you don't apply yourself
  • COMPASS assessment: Singapore uses a points-based COMPASS framework to assess EP applications

Understanding COMPASS (EP Points System)

Since 2023, all EP applications are assessed using the COMPASS framework. Your employer needs to score 40+ points across 4 categories:

CategoryMax PointsWhat It Measures
Salary20How your salary compares to locals in same sector
Qualifications20Quality and relevance of your degree
Diversity20Whether your nationality improves company diversity
Support for local employment20Company's track record of hiring locals

Key implication for Indians: The Diversity criterion means that if a company already has many Indian employees, your application may score lower. Companies with a more diverse workforce can award you full diversity points.

Industries Hiring Indian Professionals

  • Technology: Software engineers, data scientists, cloud architects — Google, Meta, Grab, Sea Group
  • Banking & Finance: Risk analysts, quants, compliance — DBS, UOB, OCBC, Citi, Goldman Sachs
  • Consulting: McKinsey, BCG, Deloitte, PwC Singapore offices
  • Biomedical: Growing sector, especially post-COVID pharmaceutical investment

S Pass: For Mid-Level Professionals

If your salary is below SGD $5,000 but above SGD $3,150, you may qualify for an S Pass instead:

  • Minimum salary: SGD $3,150/month (SGD $3,650 for financial services)
  • Requires technical diploma or degree
  • Subject to sector quotas — employer must have headroom

Path to Permanent Residency

After 2+ years on an EP, you can apply for Singapore PR. Key factors:

  • Strong salary track record
  • Tax compliance
  • Family ties in Singapore (spouse/child who is PR or citizen helps)
  • Community involvement (volunteering, professional associations)

There's no points system for PR — it's entirely discretionary. But EP holders with 3–5 years of consistent employment at reputable companies have the highest success rates.
