Thailand is one of the most popular travel destinations for Koreans — and the process couldn't be simpler. South Korean passport holders enjoy visa-free entry for up to 30 days in Thailand. No application, no fee, no pre-approval needed. Simply board your flight and present your passport on arrival.

Entry Requirements for Koreans

  • Valid South Korean passport (minimum 6 months validity recommended)
  • Onward or return flight ticket (may be requested by immigration)
  • Proof of accommodation (hotel booking)
  • Sufficient funds: THB 10,000 (~$280) per person recommended
  • No visa application required

The 30-day stamp can be extended by 30 more days at any Thai immigration office for THB 1,900 (~$53), giving a total stay of 60 days without leaving the country.

Popular Destinations for Korean Travelers

  • Bangkok — temples (Wat Pho, Wat Arun), street food, rooftop bars, massive malls
  • Phuket & Krabi — beaches, diving, island hopping — direct flights from Incheon
  • Chiang Mai — temple trekking, elephant sanctuaries, night bazaar
  • Koh Samui / Koh Tao — luxury resorts and diving spots
  • Pattaya — popular with Korean tour groups

Tips for Korean Travelers

  • Download the KakaoMap Thailand overlay — Korean mapping works in Bangkok
  • Korean food is widely available in Bangkok's Sukhumvit area (Soi 12 has Korean restaurants)
  • Use Grab — it's like KakaoTaxi and much cheaper than taxis flagged on the street
  • Avoid tap water — stick to bottled water throughout your stay
  • Best travel seasons: November–February (cool and dry); avoid April–May (hottest), June–October (rainy season)
  • Carry some Thai Baht cash — smaller markets, temples, and tuk-tuks don't accept cards
