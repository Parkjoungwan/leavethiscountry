Australia is one of the most popular destinations for young Koreans seeking work experience abroad. The Working Holiday Visa (Subclass 417) is available to Korean nationals aged 18–35, offers complete work freedom, and can be extended up to 3 years for those who complete regional work. Australia's high minimum wage makes it an excellent way to earn and save while experiencing a new culture.

Extending to 2nd and 3rd Year WHV

  • Second year (Subclass 417): complete 88 days of specified regional work (farming, fishing, mining, construction) in regional Australia
  • Third year (Subclass 417): complete 179 days of specified work in a designated regional area
  • Regional areas pay well — fruit picking can earn AUD $800–$1,500/week

Skilled Worker Pathway (Subclass 482 or 189)

For Korean professionals with work experience, the Temporary Skills Shortage (TSS) Visa (Subclass 482) and skilled migration routes offer a longer-term pathway:

  • Subclass 482: employer-sponsored, up to 4 years, requires job offer in a shortage occupation
  • Subclass 189: permanent residency through points-based skilled migration (Expression of Interest)
  • In-demand occupations for Koreans: engineering, IT, nursing, accounting, construction

Key Tips for Korean Workers

  • Apply for WHV online through the ImmiAccount portal — entirely online, no in-person visit needed
  • Sydney and Melbourne have large Korean communities — Korean restaurants, churches, and social networks are well-established
  • Tax File Number (TFN): apply online through the ATO as soon as you arrive — needed for any employment
  • Regional work for the 2nd year is physically demanding but pays well — plan for it if you want to extend
  • Australia's Superannuation (retirement fund) is mandatory — claim it back when you leave if not staying permanently
