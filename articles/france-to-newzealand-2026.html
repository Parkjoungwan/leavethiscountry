Main Work Visa: PVT Working Holiday / AEWV

Working Holiday (PVT) allows 23 months work+travel. AEWV for accredited employer roles. French professionals in-demand. Processing typically takes 14–28 days.

Key Requirements

  • Valid passport (minimum 6 months validity beyond intended stay)
  • Job offer from New Zealand employer (most routes)
  • Relevant qualifications and work experience
  • Language proficiency (English and/or local language)
  • Health insurance coverage

Tips for France Applicants

Nationals from France should research sector-specific demand in New Zealand before applying. Professional credential recognition, language requirements, and sponsor availability vary widely by industry. LinkedIn and country-specific job boards are the best starting point for identifying sponsoring employers.

Main Study Visa: Student Visa

Student visa easy for French nationals. PVT Working Holiday available. Post-study work visa 3yr. Auckland popular with French students. Processing typically takes 14 days.

Tuition and Costs

Annual costs for international students from France in New Zealand are approximately NZD 25K–40K/yr. This includes tuition but not always accommodation and living expenses. Scholarship opportunities at public institutions are available — research university-specific financial aid early.

Application Checklist

  • Acceptance letter from a recognized institution in New Zealand
  • Proof of financial means (tuition + living costs)
  • Language proficiency test (IELTS/TOEFL or local equivalent)
  • Academic transcripts and diplomas (apostilled/notarized)
  • Health insurance covering the full duration of study
  • Valid passport (minimum 6 months beyond intended stay)

Post-Study Work Rights

Many countries offer post-study work permits allowing graduates to gain local work experience, which can be a pathway to longer-term residence. Check the specific post-study visa availability for New Zealand to plan your career pathway in advance.

Main Immigration Pathway: Skilled Migrant Resident Visa

Skilled Migrant points-based. French professionals strong candidates. 5yr residency → citizenship. Expected processing timeline: 12–18 months.

Eligibility Criteria

  • Typically requires prior legal residence or work experience in New Zealand
  • Language proficiency in the official language(s)
  • Financial stability (income, savings, or employment proof)
  • Clean criminal record and health standards
  • Integration requirements (civics test, language test) in some countries

Long-Term Pathways for France Nationals

For France passport holders, New Zealand offers achievable but competitive immigration options. Building strong local ties — employment history, community involvement, and language skills — significantly increases success rates and may accelerate processing timelines.

Entry Requirement: NZeTA (NZD 23)

Practical Travel Information

  • Weekly living costs: approximately $300 USD (budget traveler)
  • Cheapest direct flights: from $1,200 USD one-way (varies by season)
  • Best booking time: 6–8 weeks in advance for best fares
  • Currency: check current exchange rates before departure

New Zealand warmly welcomes travelers from France. The straightforward entry process means you can focus on planning your itinerary.
