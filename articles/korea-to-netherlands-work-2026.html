Orientation Year Visa — A Unique Advantage

If you graduated from a Dutch university or one of the QS/THE top-50 world universities (which includes several Korean universities), the Orientation Year Visa lets you live in the Netherlands for 1 year to find work — without needing a job offer first.

  • Valid 1 year; cannot be renewed
  • Can work any job while searching for an HSM-qualifying position
  • Fee: €350
  • Korean universities that may qualify: KAIST, Seoul National University, POSTECH (check current year's eligibility list)

Dutch Tech Ecosystem

  • ASML (Eindhoven): world's only EUV chip machine maker — significant Korean semiconductor industry overlap
  • Booking.com, Adyen (Amsterdam): large tech employers with international teams
  • Shell, Philips, NXP: energy, healthcare tech, semiconductors
  • Samsung SDI and SK Innovation have European battery operations connected to Dutch logistics hubs

Key Tips

  • Dutch directness can feel blunt to Koreans — it's a cultural norm, not rudeness
  • Cycling is the primary local transport — buy a second-hand bike immediately
  • Healthcare requires mandatory insurance (€140–€160/month); your employer may partially cover it
  • The Netherlands has a growing Korean community, especially in Amsterdam and Eindhoven
  • DigiD (digital citizen ID) is needed for taxes, healthcare, and government services — apply early
