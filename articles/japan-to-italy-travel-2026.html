Italy is one of Japan's most beloved travel destinations — a country that shares Japan's deep reverence for craftsmanship, food culture, history, and aesthetics. Japanese passport holders can visit Italy and the entire Schengen Area visa-free for 90 days within any 180-day period. No embassy visit, no waiting — perfect for spontaneous long-haul escapes.

Entry Requirements for Japanese Travelers

  • Valid Japanese passport (minimum 3 months beyond planned return)
  • ETIAS travel authorization (when fully launched): €7, valid 3 years, processed online
  • Proof of accommodation and return flight
  • Sufficient funds (€100/day recommended for Italy)
  • EU travel insurance recommended (not legally required)

Japan-Italy: Shared Cultural Values

  • Both countries revere artisan craftsmanship — Italian leather and ceramics, Japanese metalwork and ceramics share a common obsession with perfection
  • Food culture is paramount in both societies — eating well is considered a civic responsibility
  • Fashion: Tokyo and Milan are two of the world's four fashion capitals
  • Ferrari, Lamborghini, and Alfa Romeo are extremely popular among Japanese car enthusiasts
  • Japanese media (manga, anime) deeply penetrated Italian culture since the 1970s–80s

Top Destinations for Japanese Visitors

  • Rome — Colosseum, Vatican, Pantheon, Campo de' Fiori — 3+ days needed
  • Florence — Uffizi Gallery, Duomo, Ponte Vecchio, artisan leather market
  • Venice — unique island city, gondolas, glass-blowing in Murano — avoid July/August crowds
  • Amalfi Coast — Positano, Ravello, Capri — best April–June, September–October
  • Cinque Terre — five colorful coastal villages, hiking trails, stunning photography
  • Sicily — ancient Greek ruins, Mt. Etna, authentic Southern Italian cuisine

Japan-Italy Schengen Strategy

Italy is Schengen — your 90-day allowance covers all 27 Schengen countries. Smart travelers combine Italy with France (TGV from Paris), Austria (train from Venice), or Spain (budget airline) for a longer European tour within the 90-day window.
