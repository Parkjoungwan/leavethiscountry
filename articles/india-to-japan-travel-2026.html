Japan is consistently one of the most sought-after travel destinations for Indians — and the good news is that getting a Japanese tourist visa is straightforward in 2026. Unlike visa-free countries, Indian passport holders do require a sticker visa, but the process is well-organized and approvals are high for applicants with clean travel history and stable finances.

How to Apply for a Japan Tourist Visa from India

Applications are submitted through VFS Global (authorized by the Japan consulate). The process is entirely in-person — Japan does not currently offer an online e-visa for Indian tourists for standard short-stay visits.

  1. Book your appointment at VFS Global Japan in your city (Delhi, Mumbai, Chennai, Kolkata, Bengaluru, Hyderabad)
  2. Prepare your documents — see the list below
  3. Visit VFS and submit biometrics + documents
  4. Wait 4–7 business days for processing
  5. Collect your passport with visa sticker from VFS or opt for courier

Documents Required

  • Valid Indian passport (minimum 6 months validity)
  • Completed visa application form (from Japan consulate website)
  • Recent passport-size photographs
  • Flight itinerary (confirmed or tentative booking)
  • Hotel bookings or accommodation proof
  • Bank statements (last 3–6 months, minimum ₹2–3 lakh balance recommended)
  • Income proof: salary slips, ITR, or business documents
  • Leave letter from employer (if employed)

What Can You Do in Japan on a Tourist Visa?

A standard short-stay visa allows tourism, visiting friends, attending events, and transit. It does not allow paid work. You can stay up to 90 days per visit on a multiple-entry visa, or the duration specified on a single-entry visa.

Japan is extremely safe, well-connected by shinkansen (bullet train), and offers experiences ranging from ancient temples in Kyoto to futuristic entertainment in Tokyo. April (cherry blossom) and October–November (autumn foliage) are peak seasons — book well in advance.

Why the Difficulty Rating Is "Easy"

  • Japan consulate approval rates for Indians are consistently high when documents are in order
  • Clear, well-published requirements — no guesswork
  • No interview required in most cases
  • Multiple VFS Global centers across India make submission accessible

Tips for Indian Travelers to Japan

  • Apply at least 3–4 weeks before your trip to account for any delays
  • Book a Japan Rail Pass before leaving India — it's significantly cheaper than buying in Japan
  • Download Google Translate and Suica (IC card) apps — most signs are in Japanese only outside tourist areas
  • Cash is still widely used in Japan — carry yen in smaller denominations
  • Halal-certified restaurants and vegetarian options are available in major cities but require planning
