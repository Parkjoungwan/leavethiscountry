Malaysia is one of Southeast Asia's most accessible and rewarding destinations for Indian travelers. In 2026, Indians can enter Malaysia through the eNTL (Electronic Travel Registration and Information System) — a fast, affordable online system that allows a 30-day stay. Malaysia's large Indian Tamil community, familiar food options, and budget-friendly prices make it particularly welcoming for Indian visitors.

Note: The eNTL is recommended — it avoids any potential queuing or questioning at arrival immigration counters and provides a clear electronic record of your approved stay.

How to Apply for eNTL

  1. Visit the official eNTL portal (entri.myimms.com.my)
  2. Fill in your travel details, passport number, and flight information
  3. Pay RM 115 by credit/debit card
  4. Receive digital approval within minutes to 24 hours
  5. Show approval on your phone or as a printout at the border

Top Experiences for Indian Travelers

  • Kuala Lumpur — Petronas Twin Towers, Batu Caves, Bukit Bintang shopping
  • Penang — UNESCO heritage town, famous Indian-Muslim food street
  • Langkawi — duty-free island, beaches, cable car
  • Cameron Highlands — tea plantations, cool weather, strawberry farms
  • Malacca — colonial history, night markets

Tips for Indian Travelers

  • Malaysia has one of the largest Indian Tamil communities in the world — Tamil food is everywhere
  • Halal food is ubiquitous; vegetarian options easy to find in Indian and Chinese restaurants
  • Carry some Ringgit cash — smaller vendors may not accept cards
  • Grab (like Uber) is the best way to get around cities; meter taxis can be expensive
  • Duty-free shopping in Langkawi: alcohol, chocolates, and electronics are significantly cheaper
