Spain has emerged as a top destination for Indian tech professionals and remote workers since launching the Digital Nomad Visa in 2023. Barcelona and Madrid have established Indian IT communities, and Spain's lower cost of living relative to Northern Europe makes it attractive for professionals in finance, software, and consulting.

Digital Nomad Visa

  • Requires €2,646/month minimum remote income (200% of Spain's SMI minimum wage)
  • At least 80% of income must come from clients outside Spain
  • Valid for 1 year, renewable to 3-year residence permit
  • Apply at Spanish consulate in India (Mumbai or New Delhi); fee approximately €73
  • Required documents: employment contract or client contracts, proof of income, private health insurance covering Spain

Beckham Law Tax Benefit

  • Qualifying new residents pay flat 24% income tax on Spanish-source income (vs. progressive rates up to 47%)
  • Applicable for 6 years from arrival
  • Must not have been Spanish tax resident in past 5 years
  • Particularly beneficial for Indian IT professionals earning €60,000–€150,000+

Highly Qualified Professionals Route

  • Requires job offer from Spanish company + degree qualification
  • Tech salaries in Madrid/Barcelona: €30,000–€60,000/year
  • Spain's tech scene (Glovo, Cabify, Barcelona Mobile World Congress hub) offers growing opportunities

Spain offers some of the most affordable university education in Western Europe. For Indian students, Spain's combination of very low tuition, improving English-medium postgraduate programs, and vibrant student life in cities like Barcelona, Madrid, Valencia, and Seville makes it increasingly attractive.

Student Visa Requirements

  • Apply at Spanish consulate in India (Mumbai, New Delhi, Chennai)
  • University acceptance letter (pre-enrollment in Spain's university system)
  • Proof of financial means: €600–€700/month for living expenses
  • Private health insurance for Spain
  • DELE language certificate (B2 for Spanish programs) or IELTS/TOEFL for English-medium programs

Spain offers several immigration routes suitable for Indian nationals, from passive income residency to investment visas. The pathway to EU citizenship after 10 years makes Spain attractive for long-term settlement, particularly for retirees and those with remote income or investments.

Non-Lucrative Visa (NLV)

  • For those who will NOT work in Spain — retirees, those with rental income, dividends, or substantial savings
  • Minimum income: €2,400/month for primary applicant + €600/month per dependent
  • Indian rental income, business dividends, and fixed deposits all qualify as proof
  • Private health insurance covering Spain required
  • Initial 1-year visa, renewable for 2-year periods; after 5 years → long-term residence

Golden Visa

  • €500,000 real estate investment (no mortgage), or €1 million in Spanish company shares/bank deposits, or €2 million in Spanish government bonds
  • Grants 2-year initial residence, renewable for 5 years
  • Right to live and work in Spain; family members included
  • Path to permanent residence after 5 years, citizenship after 10 years

Long-Term Residence and Citizenship

  • Permanent Residence (EU long-term resident status) after 5 continuous years
  • Spanish citizenship after 10 years legal residence for Indian nationals
  • Spanish language B1 level required for citizenship
  • Spain does not generally allow dual nationality with India (India doesn't permit dual citizenship)

Indian passport holders require a Schengen visa to enter Spain. The visa covers all 27 Schengen Area countries, so getting a Spain Schengen visa allows travel to France, Germany, Italy, Portugal, and more on the same trip. Apply well in advance — peak summer processing times (June–August) can stretch to 4–6 weeks.

Schengen Visa for Spain

  • Fee: €80 (adults), €40 (ages 6–11), free (under 6)
  • Apply at: Spanish consulate or VFS Global Spain offices in Mumbai, Delhi, Chennai, Bangalore, Kolkata, Hyderabad
  • Processing time: 15–30 days (apply at least 6 weeks in advance in peak season)
  • Required: valid passport (6 months+), travel insurance (€30,000 minimum), hotel/accommodation bookings, return flights, 3-month bank statements, employment/income proof
  • Valid for 90 days within any 180-day period across all Schengen countries
