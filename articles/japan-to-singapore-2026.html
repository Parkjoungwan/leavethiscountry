Employment Pass (EP)

The Employment Pass (EP) is Singapore's primary work visa for professionals, managers, executives, and technicians. As of 2025, the minimum qualifying salary is SGD 5,000 per month (SGD 5,500 for the financial services sector), with higher thresholds for older, more experienced candidates. Applications are submitted by the employing company through the Ministry of Manpower's (MOM) EP Online portal. The EP is typically issued for 1–2 years and is renewable. It also allows the holder to sponsor family members (dependants) on a Dependant's Pass.

Singapore is a major hub for Japanese multinational corporations operating in Southeast Asia. Companies such as SoftBank, Sony, Toyota, Panasonic, and Mitsubishi have regional headquarters or significant operations in Singapore. This creates a substantial demand for Japanese-speaking professionals — both for regional management roles and Japan-facing business development positions. Knowledge of English is essential for most EP roles, as Singapore's working language is English.

COMPASS Framework

Since September 2023, new EP applications are evaluated under the COMPASS (Complementarity Assessment Framework) points-based system. Candidates must score at least 40 points across criteria including salary benchmark against local peers, qualifications, diversity contribution to the company's workforce, and whether the employer is on a strategic economic priority list. Japanese candidates with strong qualifications from reputable universities and competitive salaries typically score well. Employers in sectors with few Japanese nationals can also benefit from the diversity bonus.

S Pass and Other Options

  • S Pass: For mid-skilled workers earning at least SGD 3,150/month; subject to quota restrictions per employer
  • Personalised Employment Pass (PEP): For high earners (last drawn SGD 22,500+/month); not tied to a single employer
  • ONE Pass (Overseas Networks & Expertise): For top global talent earning SGD 30,000+/month or with outstanding achievements

Top Universities and Admission

Singapore punches far above its weight in higher education. The National University of Singapore (NUS) and Nanyang Technological University (NTU) consistently rank among the world's top 20 universities. SMU (Singapore Management University) is highly regarded for business and law. All three institutions teach primarily in English, making them accessible to Japanese students with strong English proficiency. However, competition for admission is intense — NUS and NTU typically receive tens of thousands of applications for limited international student places.

Annual tuition fees for international students (without Singapore government subsidies) range from SGD 17,000 to SGD 40,000 per year depending on program and institution. NUS and NTU offer tuition fee grants to international students who commit to working in Singapore for 3 years after graduation, effectively reducing tuition to SGD 8,000–17,000 per year — a significant saving.

Student's Pass and Living Costs

All international students must obtain a Student's Pass from the Immigration and Checkpoints Authority (ICA) after receiving an offer from an ICA-registered institution. The application is completed through the SOLAR (Student's Pass On-Line Application and Registration) system. Living costs in Singapore are high — estimate SGD 1,500–2,500 per month for accommodation, food, and transport. University halls of residence are available but competitive. Japanese students should apply for on-campus housing as early as possible. Singapore's small geographic size makes commuting manageable by MRT (metro system).

Scholarships

  • ASEAN Undergraduate Scholarship: Full tuition + allowance — Japanese students are not ASEAN but check university-specific awards
  • NUS Merit Scholarships: Available to outstanding international undergraduates
  • SINGA (Singapore International Graduate Award): Fully-funded PhD scholarships in science and engineering

Permanent Residency via Employment Pass

Singapore Permanent Residency (PR) is highly sought after and difficult to obtain. The primary pathway for Japanese professionals is through the Permanent Residence scheme for EP or S Pass holders. The ICA does not publish explicit criteria or a points threshold — PR is assessed holistically based on economic contribution, family ties, length of stay, and integration potential. Most successful applicants have held an EP for at least 2–3 years, demonstrate stable high income, and have genuine ties to Singapore. Rejection is common even for long-term residents, and reapplying after 6 months is possible.

Global Investor Programme (GIP)

Wealthy Japanese nationals can pursue Singapore PR through the Global Investor Programme (GIP), which grants PR in exchange for significant investment in Singapore businesses or funds. The minimum investment is SGD 10 million (previously SGD 2.5 million, revised upward significantly in 2023). This pathway is used by ultra-high-net-worth individuals and is not applicable to most ordinary migrants.

Citizenship Considerations

Singapore citizenship can be applied for after holding PR for at least 2 years. Singapore generally does not require renunciation of previous citizenship during the PR stage, but Singapore citizenship requires renouncing all other citizenships. Since Japan also does not permit dual nationality in most cases, Japanese nationals who naturalise in Singapore must give up their Japanese passport. Singapore citizens gain access to excellent public housing (HDB), CPF (pension fund) contributions, and children's subsidised education — strong incentives for those committed to long-term settlement.

Visa-Free Entry

Japanese passport holders can enter Singapore visa-free for up to 30 days. No pre-arrival authorization is needed. Upon arrival at Changi Airport, immigration officers typically grant 30 days' social visit pass, extendable at the ICA building for an additional 30 days if needed. Singapore's Changi Airport is consistently rated the world's best and serves as a major Southeast Asian hub, with numerous daily direct flights from Tokyo (Narita and Haneda) on Singapore Airlines, JAL, ANA, and Scoot. Flight time is approximately 7 hours.

Singapore as a Base for Southeast Asia

Singapore's strategic location and excellent connectivity make it an ideal base for exploring Southeast Asia. From Singapore's Changi Airport, flights to Bangkok, Bali, Kuala Lumpur, and Ho Chi Minh City are under 2–3 hours. Singapore itself is compact and can be explored thoroughly in 3–5 days. Key attractions include Gardens by the Bay, Marina Bay Sands, Sentosa Island, Hawker food centres (Lau Pa Sat, Maxwell), and the cultural neighborhoods of Chinatown, Little India, and Kampong Glam.

Practical Travel Information

  • Currency: Singapore Dollar (SGD); 1 SGD ≈ 110 JPY (2026)
  • Language: English is the primary language — very accessible for Japanese visitors with English skills
  • Climate: Tropical year-round (28–33°C); bring light clothing and expect frequent afternoon rain showers
  • MRT and bus network is excellent; EZ-Link card covers all public transport
  • Chewing gum, littering, jaywalking, and durian on public transport are regulated — fines apply
