Japan and the United States have one of the world's deepest bilateral economic relationships — more than 1,000 Japanese companies operate in the US, and 70,000+ Japanese nationals live and work in California alone. This creates real, practical pathways for Japanese professionals to work in the US through company transfers, treaty visas, and talent visas — even when the H-1B lottery doesn't cooperate.

L-1 Visa: Japanese Company Transfer Route

The L-1 visa is a particularly strong option for Japanese professionals employed at major Japanese corporations with US offices. Toyota, Sony, Mitsubishi, Panasonic, Nintendo — all of these companies regularly transfer Japanese staff to their US operations.

  • Requires at least 1 year of employment with the company outside the US within the last 3 years
  • L-1A: for managers and executives → can lead directly to EB-1C Green Card
  • L-1B: for specialized knowledge workers
  • Processing: 2–4 months (or 2 weeks with premium processing)
  • Fee: $460–$730 (employer pays)

US Japanese Community

Japan's VWP Access: Travel Without Visa

Japanese nationals can visit the US for 90 days without a visa under the Visa Waiver Program (ESTA, $21). This is convenient for job interviews or short business trips — but does not allow working while in the US on ESTA status.
