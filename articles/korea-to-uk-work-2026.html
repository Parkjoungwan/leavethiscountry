The UK offers two main work routes for Korean nationals: the Youth Mobility Scheme (YMS) for those aged 18–30, and the Skilled Worker Visa for those with a job offer. South Korea has a bilateral Youth Mobility agreement with the UK, making it straightforward for young Koreans to live and work in the UK for up to 2 years with full work freedom.

The YMS is a one-time opportunity — you cannot apply again after using it. It's best used strategically to build UK work experience and potentially transition to a Skilled Worker visa.

Option 2: Skilled Worker Visa

  • Requires a job offer from a UK-licensed sponsor employer
  • Minimum salary: £38,700/year (general threshold from April 2024)
  • Visa fee: £719 (up to 3 years) or £1,420 (over 3 years)
  • IHS: £1,035/year (NHS access)
  • Valid for duration of employment + 14 days
  • After 5 years: eligible for Indefinite Leave to Remain (ILR = permanent residency)

  • London has a large Korean community — New Malden (southwest London) is known as "Korea Town"

Key Tips for Korean Workers

  • Apply for YMS before turning 31 — it's one of the best entry points for building UK career history
  • Open a UK bank account early — Monzo, Revolut, or Starling are easiest for newcomers without UK credit history
  • NI (National Insurance) number: apply as soon as you arrive — needed for employment and tax
  • NHS access is included in the IHS — use it; private healthcare in the UK is expensive
  • K-pop and Korean culture are extremely popular in the UK — leverage this for cultural and networking opportunities
