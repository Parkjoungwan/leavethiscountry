Singapore is a natural stepping stone for Japanese professionals seeking international experience within Asia. The city-state is home to over 30,000 Japanese residents, has dozens of Japanese companies with regional headquarters, and offers a familiar safe, orderly environment alongside world-class salaries. Singapore's Employment Pass system is employer-sponsored, making it essential to secure a job offer before applying.

Employment Pass (EP) for Japanese Nationals

  • Employer submits application via MOM's EP Online portal
  • Minimum salary: SGD $5,000/month for general applicants
  • Assessment includes COMPASS (points system) — education, salary, company diversity all scored
  • Processing: 3–8 weeks
  • Initial validity: 2 years (first EP), renewable for 3 years
  • Dependant Pass (for family) available if salary exceeds SGD $6,000/month

Japanese Companies in Singapore

  • Sony, Panasonic, Fujitsu: APAC headquarters in Singapore
  • Sumitomo, Mitsubishi, Mitsui, ITOCHU: major trading house Singapore offices
  • Toyota, Nissan: regional distribution and commercial divisions
  • Nomura, Daiwa, MUFG: Japanese banking and finance regional hubs
  • Singapore's Little Japan (Orchard Road area) has Japanese groceries, restaurants, and cultural organizations

Key Tips for Japanese Workers

  • Japanese language skills are particularly valued in Japan-facing business roles — leverage this
  • Singapore's work culture blends Western efficiency with Asian relationship-building — familiar to Japanese professionals
  • CPF (mandatory savings) does not apply to EP holders — your full salary is take-home minus income tax
  • Singapore income tax is progressive but low — 0% on first SGD $20,000, max 24% on very high incomes
  • Housing in Singapore is expensive — expect SGD $2,500–$4,000/month for a 1BR in central areas
