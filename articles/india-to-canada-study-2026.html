Canada remains one of the most popular destinations for Indian students in 2026 — and for good reason. The country offers globally recognized degrees, a clear path to permanent residency through the Post-Graduation Work Permit (PGWP), and one of the largest Indian diaspora communities in the world. But it's not cheap, and the application process has become more competitive over recent years.

Note: The GIC (Guaranteed Investment Certificate) deposit of CAD $20,635 is a requirement introduced by IRCC. This money is held in a Canadian bank and released to you in monthly installments once you arrive. It is not a fee — you get it back.

The Canadian Student Visa (Study Permit) Process

To study in Canada, Indian students need a Study Permit from Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC). Here are the key steps:

  1. Get a Letter of Acceptance (LOA) from a Designated Learning Institution (DLI) in Canada
  2. Apply online at IRCC's website with your LOA, financial proof, and biometrics
  3. Pay the GIC deposit (CAD $20,635) at a participating Canadian bank
  4. Submit biometrics at your nearest VAC (Visa Application Centre)
  5. Wait for processing — currently averaging 8–16 weeks
  6. Get your Port of Entry (POE) letter and travel to Canada

Post-Study Work: The PGWP Advantage

One of Canada's biggest draws is the Post-Graduation Work Permit (PGWP). After completing a program of 8 months or longer at a DLI, you can apply for an open work permit:

  • Programs under 2 years: PGWP valid for same duration as your program
  • Programs 2 years or longer: PGWP valid for 3 years

The PGWP gives you the freedom to work for any Canadian employer, which is the critical first step toward Express Entry permanent residency.

Difficulty Rating: Why Moderate?

Canada's student visa is rated Moderate for Indian applicants because:

  • Rejection rates have risen due to high application volumes — IRCC has tightened scrutiny since 2024
  • You must prove "genuine intent to leave" Canada after your studies (though the PGWP pathway complicates this)
  • Strong proof of funds is essential — bank statements, GIC, and sponsor letters all matter
  • Academic scores and English proficiency (IELTS 6.0+ typically required) are evaluated

Key Tips for Indian Applicants in 2026

  • Apply to a DLI on the IRCC-approved DLI list
  • Apply as early as possible — processing times vary widely by season
  • Have your GIC set up before applying — it significantly strengthens your application
  • Prepare a strong Statement of Purpose explaining why you chose Canada and your study program
  • IELTS Academic score of 6.5+ overall improves approval chances
