New Zealand is a popular destination for young Koreans seeking English immersion, outdoor adventure, and international work experience. South Korea has a Working Holiday Agreement with New Zealand, allowing Korean nationals aged 18–30 to live and work freely for 12 months. New Zealand's relatively small population creates genuine skills shortages in healthcare, IT, and construction — offering a clear pathway to permanent residency for skilled workers.

Skilled Migrant Category (SMC): Long-Term Route

For those seeking permanent residency, New Zealand's Skilled Migrant Category is a points-based system:

  • Points awarded for: age, qualifications, skilled employment offer in NZ, work experience
  • Minimum 160 points to submit an Expression of Interest (EOI)
  • Job offer in a shortage occupation significantly boosts points
  • Processing: ~12–18 months from EOI to PR

In-Demand Occupations

  • Registered Nurses and healthcare workers
  • Civil and structural engineers
  • Software developers and IT professionals
  • Electricians, plumbers, construction trades
  • Teachers (primary and secondary)

Key Tips for Korean Workers

  • Auckland has the largest Korean community in NZ — Korean food, churches, and social groups well-established
  • New Zealand English accents are familiar from Korean media — adjustment is easier than expected
  • IRD (Inland Revenue) number: apply online as soon as you arrive — needed for employment
  • New Zealand's ACC (Accident Compensation Corporation) covers injuries at work automatically — no separate insurance needed
  • Housing costs in Auckland are high; Christchurch and Wellington offer more affordable options
