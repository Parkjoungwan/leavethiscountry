Italy is home to some of Europe's oldest and most respected universities — Politecnico di Milano, Università di Bologna, and La Sapienza being among the top-ranked globally. For Indian students, Italy offers low tuition at public universities, a rich cultural experience, and a gateway to the Schengen area. The visa process requires careful documentation but is manageable with preparation.

Key advantage: Italian public university tuition is income-based (ISEE assessment). Many Indian students pay very low tuition — sometimes as low as €156/year — based on declared income levels.

The Dichiarazione di Valore: Critical Document

Italy requires a Dichiarazione di Valore (Declaration of Value) for your Indian academic certificates. This is a document issued by the Italian consulate in India confirming that your educational qualification is recognized in Italy. It takes time to prepare and is often the bottleneck in the process.

Student Visa Application Steps

  1. Apply and get admitted to an Italian university (via Universitaly portal for pre-enrollment)
  2. Get your Indian degree certificates apostilled and translated into Italian
  3. Request Dichiarazione di Valore from Italian consulate in India (Mumbai, New Delhi, or Kolkata)
  4. Apply for Type D (long-stay) student visa at the Italian consulate with full documentation
  5. Pay €50 visa fee
  6. Travel to Italy and apply for a Permesso di Soggiorno (residence permit) within 8 days of arrival

Work Rights During Study

  • Student visa holders can work up to 20 hours per week
  • Summer and holiday periods allow more hours with employer agreement
  • Italy's labor market is smaller than Germany/UK — most student jobs are in hospitality or tutoring

Key Tips

  • Apply through the Universitaly pre-enrollment portal — mandatory for most public universities
  • Start the Dichiarazione di Valore process 6+ months before your intended start date
  • Learn basic Italian — while many master's programs are English-taught, daily life requires Italian
  • Book accommodation early, especially in Milan and Rome — student housing is very competitive
  • Regional scholarship programs (DSU) offer free meals, accommodation, and cash grants to qualifying students
