Spain attracts Hong Kong residents seeking a warmer, slower-paced European lifestyle with access to the full EU. Spain's Mediterranean climate, world-class cuisine, rich culture, and affordable living costs (compared to Western Europe) make it particularly appealing. For retired HK professionals and those with remote income, Spain's Non-Lucrative Visa is the most accessible route; for remote workers, the new Digital Nomad Visa (launched 2023) is ideal.

Non-Lucrative Visa (NLV) — For Retirees and Those with Passive Income

  • Designed for those with sufficient passive income who will NOT work in Spain
  • Income proof: €2,400/month for primary applicant; €600/month per dependent
  • HK rental income, dividends, pensions, or savings withdrawals all qualify
  • Must have private health insurance covering Spain
  • Apply at Spanish consulate in Hong Kong with full documentation
  • Initial 1-year visa; renew for 2-year periods

Spain's Beckham Law — Tax Benefit

The "Beckham Law" (Special Expatriate Tax Regime) allows qualifying individuals to pay a flat 24% income tax rate (instead of progressive rates up to 47%) on Spanish-source income for 6 years. For HK finance professionals earning €80,000–€200,000+, this is a substantial benefit:

  • Must become Spanish tax resident
  • Must not have been tax resident in Spain in past 5 years
  • Applicable to: Digital Nomad visa holders, Skilled Worker visa, relocated executives

Barcelona vs. Madrid for HK Expats

  • Barcelona: Mediterranean coast, Gaudí architecture, beach lifestyle, strong international expat community, Catalan culture alongside Spanish
  • Madrid: Spanish capital, world-class museums, central location for Spain/Europe travel, more conservative but also more cosmopolitan in business
  • Both have Chinese communities, Chinese supermarkets, and some Cantonese-speaking residents
  • Cost of living considerably lower than Hong Kong — a couple can live comfortably on €3,000–€4,000/month
