The United States has a large, established Hong Kong diaspora community — particularly in the San Francisco Bay Area, New York, and Los Angeles. For HK finance, tech, and business professionals, the US remains a top destination despite the challenging visa process. HKSAR passport holders qualify for the Visa Waiver Program (ESTA), allowing visa-free visits for 90 days — useful for job interviews, business trips, or exploring before committing.

HK-US Relations & Visa Impact

Since 2020, US–HK relations have shifted significantly. Key changes for HK residents:

  • HKSAR passport holders still qualify for ESTA — can visit 90 days no visa
  • US suspended its extradition treaty with HK (2020) — no change for lawful visitors/workers
  • US government created specific refugee and humanitarian provisions for certain HK activists — but not applicable to typical professional emigrants
  • No special work visa stream exists for HK residents (unlike UK/Canada/Australia)

L-1 Route: The Most Reliable Path

For HK professionals working at multinational firms with US offices, the L-1 intracompany transfer is far more reliable than the H-1B lottery:

  • Must have worked at the qualifying company for 1+ year within the last 3 years
  • Company must have US operations (existing or new office)
  • No annual cap — can apply year-round
  • L-1A (manager/executive) leads to EB-1C Green Card without labor certification
  • Major HK companies with US offices: HSBC, Standard Chartered, CK Hutchison, Swire, Li & Fung

HK Community in the US

  • San Francisco / Oakland: historically the largest HK community in the US; Chinatown and Richmond District have Cantonese-speaking groceries, dim sum, and community organizations
  • New York: Flushing Queens and Manhattan Chinatown; major finance community
  • Los Angeles: Monterey Park, San Gabriel Valley — significant Cantonese community
  • Post-2021 wave of HK emigrants increasingly to suburban tech hubs (Silicon Valley, Seattle, Austin)
