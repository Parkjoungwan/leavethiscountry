The USA hosts the largest number of Indian international students in the world. Despite the high cost and competitive visa process, the combination of world-class universities, STEM OPT work authorization, and industry connections makes the US a top destination for Indian students targeting tech, business, and research careers.

Step-by-Step F-1 Visa Process

  1. Get admitted to a SEVP-certified US university or college
  2. Receive Form I-20 from your institution's DSO (Designated School Official)
  3. Pay SEVIS fee ($350) at fmjfee.com — must be done before scheduling interview
  4. Pay MRV fee ($185) and schedule visa interview at the US embassy/consulate in India (Delhi, Mumbai, Chennai, Hyderabad, Kolkata)
  5. Prepare DS-160 online visa application form
  6. Attend interview — bring all documents, be ready to explain your study plan
  7. Visa decision — typically same-day or 1–3 business days after interview

Post-Study Work: OPT and STEM OPT

This is the biggest draw for Indian students choosing the US:

  • OPT (Optional Practical Training): 12 months of work authorization after graduation — any employer, any field
  • STEM OPT extension: additional 24 months (total 36 months) if your degree is in Science, Technology, Engineering, or Math
  • During OPT/STEM OPT, your employer can file for H-1B work visa sponsorship

For STEM graduates especially, the 3-year OPT window provides significant runway to transition to long-term work status in the US.

Why the Difficulty Rating Is "Hard"

  • F-1 visa rejection rates have increased for Indian applicants — strong financial proof is critical
  • You must demonstrate "non-immigrant intent" — convincing the officer you plan to return to India after studies
  • US consulate interview wait times in India can stretch to weeks or months during peak seasons (May–August)
  • Applications require meticulous documentation — missing one item can delay or result in rejection

Key Tips for Indian Applicants

  • Book your visa interview slot as early as possible — slots fill up fast, especially in summer
  • Prepare a clear, honest answer about why you chose that specific university and program
  • Show strong ties to India: property, family, job offer after studies — this counters "immigrant intent" concerns
  • Financial documents must show enough to cover your entire program, not just year one
  • Many universities offer Graduate Assistantships or Teaching Assistantships that significantly reduce cost
