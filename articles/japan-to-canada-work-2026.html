Canada is one of the most popular destinations for young Japanese professionals and students. The International Experience Canada (IEC) Working Holiday Program allows Japanese nationals aged 18–35 to live and work freely in Canada for up to 12 months. Japan also has one of the highest-scoring nationalities in Canada's Express Entry system, making long-term permanent residency genuinely attainable.

Express Entry: Path to Permanent Residency

After working in Canada (often starting with IEC), many Japanese nationals transition to permanent residency through Express Entry:

  • Federal Skilled Worker (FSW): for those with skilled work experience and education
  • Canadian Experience Class (CEC): for those with at least 1 year of Canadian work experience
  • Japanese passport holders score well due to strong education, language scores, and work experience
  • Processing time: 6 months for PR after Invitation to Apply (ITA)

Japanese Community in Canada

  • Vancouver: historically the largest Japanese-Canadian community — Japantown in Powell Street area
  • Toronto: growing Japanese population, Japanese restaurants and grocery stores (T&T, Japanese marts)
  • Whistler: popular among Japanese working holiday makers for ski resort employment
  • Japanese language skills valued in tourism, hospitality, and trade roles

In-Demand Skills for Japanese Workers

  • Software engineering and IT (bilingual Japanese-English roles)
  • Engineering (automotive, manufacturing — Toyota/Honda have Canadian operations)
  • Hospitality and tourism (Japanese guest services)
  • Finance and accounting
  • Nursing and healthcare (with Canadian credential recognition)

Key Tips

  • Apply for IEC as soon as the pool opens — quotas fill quickly for popular nationalities like Japan
  • Take English proficiency tests (IELTS/CELPIP) before arriving — scores needed for Express Entry
  • Canadian winters are extreme in most cities — prepare appropriately for the climate
  • SIN (Social Insurance Number): apply online or in-person at Service Canada on arrival — needed for all employment
