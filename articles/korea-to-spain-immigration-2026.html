Spain is emerging as a popular relocation destination for Koreans — particularly remote workers and those seeking an affordable European lifestyle. The Spanish Digital Nomad Visa (Visado para Teletrabajadores Internacionales), launched in 2023, is one of the most accessible residency routes for Korean nationals earning income from outside Spain. Digital Nomad Visa — The Best Route for Working Koreans

Spain's DNV is ideal for Koreans working remotely for Korean or international companies that are not based in Spain. It allows you to live in Spain while keeping your existing employment.

Requirements:

  • Minimum 3 months employment with current employer (or freelance clients)
  • Minimum monthly income: €2,646 (200% of Spanish minimum wage)
  • At most 20% of income can come from Spanish clients
  • Criminal record certificate (apostilled)
  • Health insurance covering Spain
  • Accommodation proof in Spain

Spain's Beckham Law: Tax Benefit

Digital nomad visa holders can benefit from Spain's "Beckham Law" (Ley Beckham) — a special tax regime that taxes only Spanish-sourced income at a flat 24% rate for the first 6 years of residency, instead of progressive rates up to 47%. This is a significant saving for high earners.

Cost of Living in Spain

  • Barcelona: €1,400–€2,200/month (rent + living)
  • Madrid: €1,300–€2,000/month
  • Valencia, Seville: €900–€1,400/month — increasingly popular among expats
  • Korean restaurants and K-beauty stores available in Barcelona and Madrid city centers

Path to Permanent Residency

  • 5 years of legal residency → apply for Long-Term Residence Permit (EU permanent residency)
  • 10 years → eligible for Spanish citizenship (dual nationality not automatic — check Korea's rules on dual citizenship)
  • DNV is renewable every 2 years while conditions are met
