Spain is a perennial favorite for Japanese travelers seeking warmth, art, architecture, and laid-back living that contrasts sharply with Japan's fast-paced urban culture. Japanese passport holders enjoy visa-free access to Spain and all Schengen countries for 90 days within any 180-day period — no appointment, no paperwork, just arrive.

Entry for Japanese Visitors

  • Valid Japanese passport (3 months beyond planned stay)
  • ETIAS travel authorization: €7 online (once fully operational in 2025–2026)
  • Return flight booking and accommodation proof
  • Sufficient funds (€100/day recommended)

Japan-Spain Connection

  • Japan and Spain share an aesthetic love for bold architecture, craft, and distinct regional identities
  • Spain received over 400,000 Japanese tourists annually pre-pandemic
  • The Camino de Santiago pilgrimage trail is extremely popular among Japanese hikers — there are dedicated Japanese-language guidebooks and facilities
  • Gaudí's work (especially Sagrada Família) has cult following in Japan — it appears regularly in Japanese anime and media
  • Spanish cuisine — paella, tapas, Jamón — resonates with Japanese appreciation for fresh, simple flavors

Top Destinations

  • Barcelona — Sagrada Família, Park Güell, Gothic Quarter, La Boqueria market — Gaudí is #1 for Japanese visitors
  • Madrid — Prado Museum, Reina Sofía (Guernica), Retiro Park, world-class gastronomy
  • Seville — flamenco, Real Alcázar palace, Giralda tower — best October–April (summers are extreme)
  • Granada — Alhambra palace complex (book months ahead), Sacromonte caves, tapas culture
  • San Sebastián — Basque Country, world's highest concentration of Michelin stars, La Concha beach
  • Camino de Santiago — 800km pilgrimage trail from the Pyrenees to Santiago — a defining life experience for many Japanese
