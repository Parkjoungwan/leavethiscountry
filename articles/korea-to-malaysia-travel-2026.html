Malaysia is one of the most generous visa-free destinations for South Korean passport holders — Koreans can enter visa-free and stay for up to 90 days without any advance application or fee. This makes Malaysia an excellent destination for both short trips and extended stays. Kuala Lumpur's modern infrastructure, diverse food scene, and lower cost of living make it a natural stopover or destination for Korean travelers.

Entry for Korean Passport Holders

  • Valid South Korean passport
  • 90 days visa-free (extendable by leaving and re-entering)
  • Return or onward flight ticket recommended
  • No registration or fee required
  • Can be used multiple times per year

K-Culture in Malaysia

Malaysia has a passionate K-pop and Korean drama fan base — one of the largest in Southeast Asia. Korean travelers will find:

  • Dedicated Korean restaurant zones in KL's Ampang area ("Little Korea")
  • Korean beauty (K-beauty) products widely sold in pharmacies and malls
  • Korean language learning communities and cultural exchange events
  • Korean pop-up events and SM/YG/HYBE fan meetings held regularly in KL

Must-Visit Destinations

  • Kuala Lumpur — Petronas Towers, KLCC park, Bukit Bintang, Batu Caves
  • Penang — UNESCO heritage town, street food capital of Asia
  • Langkawi — duty-free island, beaches, cable car over tropical jungle
  • Cameron Highlands — tea plantations, cooler highland climate
  • Borneo (Sabah/Sarawak) — orangutans, rainforest trekking, diving

Tips for Korean Travelers

  • Malaysia is predominantly Muslim — dress modestly in religious sites and smaller towns
  • Halal food is widespread; non-halal Chinese and Indian restaurants also abundant
  • Grab is the dominant ride-hailing app — cheaper and more reliable than taxis
  • Currency: RM can be exchanged at money changers (better rates than banks) in malls and airports
  • Weather: hot and humid year-round; east coast (Terengganu, Perhentian Islands) has monsoon Nov–Mar
