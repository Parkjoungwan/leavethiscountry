France is an increasingly popular study destination for Indian students — and for good reason. French public universities charge among the lowest tuition in the developed world (as low as €170/year for EU-rate programs), and the country offers a gateway into the Schengen area. English-taught master's programs have expanded significantly, making a French degree accessible without fluency in French.

The Campus France Requirement

Unlike most countries, France requires Indian students to register with Campus France India before applying for a visa. This is an official French government body that processes and evaluates student applications.

  1. Create a Campus France account on the Études en France platform
  2. Apply to universities directly through the platform or via Parcoursup/university websites
  3. Get a Campus France acceptance (pré-inscription or dossier approval)
  4. Pay Campus France fee (~€200)
  5. Apply for VLS-TS student visa at the French consulate with your Campus France dossier
  6. Receive visa in 6–10 weeks
  7. Validate visa online within 3 months of arriving in France via ANEF portal

Working During Your Studies

French student visas allow you to work up to 964 hours per year (about 18.5 hours per week) without needing a separate work permit. This is one of the most generous student work allowances in Europe.

Post-Study Options in France

  • Autorisation Provisoire de Séjour (APS): 12-month job-search visa after master's or higher degree
  • Talent Passport: for high-skill workers and entrepreneurs after finding a job
  • France has active recruitment in tech, engineering, and data science sectors

Key Tips for Indian Applicants

  • Apply to Campus France 6–8 months before your intended start date — the process is slow
  • Many top programs are English-taught at master's level — check each university's course catalog
  • OFII registration in France (within 3 months of arrival) is mandatory for all long-stay visa holders
  • CAF housing allowance (APL) can reduce rent by €100–€200/month if you qualify
  • Consider cities like Lyon, Toulouse, Bordeaux, or Montpellier for lower cost of living than Paris
