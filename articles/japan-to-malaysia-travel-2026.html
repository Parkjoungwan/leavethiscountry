Malaysia offers Japanese visitors one of the most generous visa-free arrangements in Southeast Asia — 90 days with no application required. Combined with a short flight time (7 hours), world-class food scene, English widely spoken, and costs a fraction of Japan's, Malaysia has become a favorite for Japanese long-stay travelers, retirees, and digital nomads exploring Southeast Asia.

Entry Requirements for Japanese

  • Valid Japanese passport (minimum 6 months validity)
  • Return or onward ticket
  • Accommodation booking (hotels, rental, or host proof)
  • No prior application or fee required
  • 90-day stay limit per entry (renewal by brief exit to Singapore or Thailand)

Malaysia My Second Home (MM2H)

For Japanese looking to stay longer than tourism allows, Malaysia My Second Home offers long-term residency (5–10 years). Japanese nationals make up one of the largest MM2H cohorts historically. Requirements (as of 2024 revision):

  • Fixed deposit: RM 1,000,000 (~¥33 million) in Malaysian bank
  • Monthly offshore income: RM 40,000 (~¥1.3 million)
  • Processing: 3–6 months

Japan-Malaysia Connection

  • Malaysia's "Look East Policy" (1980s) was modeled on Japan — Malaysian engineers trained in Japanese factories; Toyota, Honda, Yamaha, Panasonic all have major plants in Malaysia
  • Proton (national car) was developed with Mitsubishi technology
  • Japanese food ubiquitous in KL — Sushi King, Sukiya, Yoshinoya, Don Don Donki all widespread
  • Kuala Lumpur's Japanese community concentrated around Ampang and Bangsar areas

Must-Visit for Japanese Travelers

  • Kuala Lumpur — Petronas Towers, Batu Caves, Central Market, Jalan Alor food street
  • Penang — George Town UNESCO heritage, hawker food heaven, street art
  • Langkawi — tropical island, duty-free shopping, cable car, clear beaches
  • Cameron Highlands — tea plantations, cool climate, strawberry farms
  • Borneo (Sabah/Sarawak) — orangutans, Mount Kinabalu, diving at Sipadan
