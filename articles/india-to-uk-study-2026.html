The UK remains the most prestigious English-speaking study destination for Indian students after Canada, with Russell Group universities commanding global recognition. But it's also one of the most expensive — and the IHS (Immigration Health Surcharge) adds a significant hidden cost many students overlook.

What Is the IHS and Why It Matters

The Immigration Health Surcharge (IHS) is a mandatory fee that gives you access to NHS healthcare during your stay. As of 2026:

  • Rate: £1,035 per year for students (was £776, increased in 2024)
  • Paid upfront for the full visa duration at time of application
  • Example: 3-year program = £3,105 paid before you arrive

This is in addition to the £490 visa application fee. Many students budget only for tuition and living costs, then are surprised by the IHS.

The Student Visa Process

  1. Receive Confirmation of Acceptance for Studies (CAS) from your UK university
  2. Apply online — you can apply up to 6 months before your course starts
  3. Pay the visa fee (£490) and IHS (full duration upfront)
  4. Provide biometrics at UKVCAS appointment
  5. Wait up to 15 working days (priority service: 5 days for extra fee)
  6. Receive BRP (Biometric Residence Permit) after arrival in UK

English Requirement

For UK Student Visa, you need to meet the English language requirement:

  • IELTS Academic: minimum 5.5–6.5 overall (varies by institution)
  • TOEFL iBT: minimum 60–90 (varies by institution)
  • PTE Academic: minimum 42–58
  • Top universities typically require IELTS 6.5–7.0

The Graduate Visa: 2 Years After Degree

The Graduate Visa lets you stay in the UK for 2 years after completing your degree (3 years for PhD graduates) with full work rights and no employer sponsorship needed:

  • Fee: £715
  • Work type: any employer, any industry
  • Can switch to Skilled Worker visa if you get a sponsoring employer
  • India is the #1 source country for UK Graduate Visa holders
