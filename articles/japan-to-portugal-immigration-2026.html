Portugal is quietly becoming one of the most popular destinations for Japanese nationals seeking to leave Japan for a more relaxed lifestyle in Europe. The combination of affordable living costs, warm climate, English-friendly culture, and an accessible path to EU residency makes Portugal uniquely attractive. The Japan-Portugal relationship dates back to the 16th century — Japan was one of the first Asian countries to have contact with European explorers via Portugal.

Why Portugal for Japanese Nationals

  • Shared values: both cultures value politeness, cleanliness, craftsmanship, and respect for elders
  • Food culture: Portuguese cuisine — fresh seafood, grilled fish, pastries — resonates strongly with Japanese food preferences
  • Safety: Portugal is consistently ranked among the world's safest countries
  • Cost of living: significantly lower than Tokyo — a couple can live well on €2,000–€2,500/month outside Lisbon
  • EU base: Portuguese residency allows travel and eventual citizenship across the EU

Japanese Community in Portugal

The Japanese community in Portugal is small but growing — particularly in Lisbon and Cascais. Japanese restaurants, grocery stores (selling Japanese imports), and cultural exchange groups have established themselves in recent years. The Portuguese are generally curious about and welcoming toward Japanese culture.

D7 Application Steps

  1. Open a Portuguese bank account (can be done remotely via Activobank or Millennium BCP)
  2. Secure accommodation in Portugal (rental contract minimum)
  3. Obtain Japanese criminal record certificate (apostilled + translated to Portuguese)
  4. Prepare proof of passive income (pension, dividends, savings, rental income)
  5. Apply at Portuguese consulate in Tokyo or Osaka
  6. Receive 4-month visa; travel to Portugal
  7. Apply at AIMA (immigration authority) for 2-year residence permit
