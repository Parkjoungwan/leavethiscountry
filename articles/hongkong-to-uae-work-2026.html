Dubai has emerged as a surprising popular choice for Hong Kong professionals seeking an alternative to migrating West. Both cities are cosmopolitan, high-income, internationally connected financial hubs with large expat populations. The UAE's zero income tax policy — similar to Hong Kong's historically low-tax environment — is a major draw, and HKSAR passport holders can visit the UAE visa-free for 30 days for job hunting purposes.

Dubai International Financial Centre (DIFC)

DIFC is Dubai's international financial district with its own legal system (based on English common law), courts, and regulatory framework. Many Hong Kong finance professionals feel at home here:

  • Major banks with DIFC offices: HSBC, Standard Chartered, JP Morgan, Goldman Sachs, MUFG
  • Both HSBC and Standard Chartered have major operations in both HK and DIFC — internal transfers common
  • Asset management, private equity, wealth management all active in DIFC
  • English common law jurisdiction — familiar to HK legal and finance professionals

UAE Work Visa Process for HK Residents

  1. HKSAR passport holders enter UAE visa-free for 30 days
  2. Receive job offer from UAE employer
  3. Employer submits work permit to MOHRE
  4. Medical test and Emirates ID registration upon arrival
  5. 2-year residence visa issued (renewable)

HK Expat Life in Dubai

  • Large Chinese community in Dubai — over 200,000 Chinese nationals (including HK); multiple Chinese restaurants and shops
  • International schools (British, American curriculum) available for children
  • No permanent residency path — UAE doesn't offer long-term PR to most expats; plan for eventual onward move
  • Lifestyle similar to HK in some ways: malls, air conditioning, dining-out culture, work-focused society
