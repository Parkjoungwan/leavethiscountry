Japan is consistently Hong Kong's #1 or #2 travel destination. HKSAR passport holders enjoy 90-day visa-free access — generous by any standard, and enough for an extended stay exploring Tokyo, the Kansai region, Hokkaido, Okinawa, and everything in between. The HK–Japan cultural connection is deep: Cantonese speakers can read many Japanese kanji, Japanese food culture has permeated Hong Kong for decades, and Hong Kongers are among Japan's most culturally knowledgeable visitors.

Entry Requirements for HK Travelers

  • Valid HKSAR passport (6 months recommended validity)
  • Return ticket and accommodation proof
  • Japan Tourist Tax: ¥1,000 per departure (collected at airport)
  • Visit Japan Web registration recommended for faster immigration processing
  • Note: BN(O) passport holders also qualify for visa-free access as UK/BOT travel document holders

HK–Japan Cultural Connection

  • Cantonese-speaking Hong Kongers can read a significant portion of Japanese kanji (traditional Chinese characters), making signs and menus partially navigable without study
  • Japanese cuisine extremely popular in HK — ramen, sushi, tonkatsu, shabu-shabu all major dining categories
  • Japanese pop culture (anime, J-pop, fashion) deeply embedded in HK youth culture
  • Major Japanese retailers (Uniqlo, Muji, Don Quijote/Donki) heavily present in HK
  • Japan has historically been HK's #1 outbound travel destination

Must-Visit for HK Travelers

  • Tokyo — Shinjuku, Shibuya, Akihabara, Harajuku, TeamLab digital art
  • Kyoto — Fushimi Inari, Arashiyama bamboo grove, geisha district Gion
  • Osaka — Dotonbori, Osaka Castle, Shinsekai, street food haven
  • Hokkaido — skiing (Niseko, Sapporo), seafood, Furano lavender fields (summer)
  • Okinawa — tropical beaches, coral reefs, distinct Ryukyu culture
  • Mt. Fuji — day trip from Tokyo via Shinkansen or bus
