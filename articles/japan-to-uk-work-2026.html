Japan and the UK have a strong bilateral Youth Mobility Scheme agreement — allowing Japanese nationals aged 18–30 to live and work freely in the UK for up to 2 years. For those outside this age range or seeking longer-term residency, the Skilled Worker visa provides a structured pathway. London's established Japanese community (particularly in North London) and the UK's Japanese business presence make the UK an accessible and welcoming destination.

Skilled Worker Visa Route

  • Requires job offer from a UK-licensed sponsor employer
  • Minimum salary: £38,700/year (2024 threshold)
  • Healthcare surcharge: £1,035/year
  • After 5 years: Indefinite Leave to Remain (permanent residency)
  • Japanese professionals particularly sought in: engineering, finance, IT, healthcare

Japanese Community in the UK

  • Over 60,000 Japanese nationals in the UK — third-largest Japanese expat community in Europe
  • Golders Green / Finchley (North London): highest concentration of Japanese residents in the UK
  • Japan Centre in Central London: Japanese grocery store, food court, and cultural hub
  • Numerous Japanese schools, weekend schools, and cultural associations throughout London
  • Chotto Matte, Umu, Sushi Tetsu — high-end Japanese dining for the community

In-Demand Skills for Japanese Workers

  • Automotive engineering (particularly EV and autonomous vehicle R&D)
  • Software development and tech roles
  • Japanese language roles in finance, law, and professional services
  • Chefs and hospitality (Japanese cuisine is extremely popular)
  • Healthcare and life sciences
